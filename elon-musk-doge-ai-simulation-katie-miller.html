Inside DOGE, AI Anxiety, and the Push to Make Life Multi-Planetary

Elon Musk’s wide-ranging conversation on the Katie Miller Podcast
Based on Katie Miller Podcast Ep.18 — “Doge, AI, Mars, and the Future of Humanity”
Elon Musk’s interview with Katie Miller moves fast, but a clear narrative emerges: a brief experiment in government efficiency, a sober view of AI’s trajectory, and a long-term bet that the most durable future for humanity is multi-planetary. This post condenses the transcript into a structured story, highlighting what Musk says he actually did in Washington, what he thinks AI will do to work, and why Starship sits in what he calls the “evolutionary hall of fame.”
Core Throughline: Eliminate waste, accelerate technological capability, and build the redundancy needed for civilization to survive.

DOGE: A Made-Up Department With Real Consequences

Musk begins with a candid admission about the origins of DOGE: it was more meme than mandate. Yet the work focused on a tangible goal—stopping payments that should never have been issued.

Doge was a made up name that had been made up, I don’t know, two or three months before and based on Internet suggestions.
— Elon Musk

From his perspective, the biggest win was procedural: require a payment code and an explanation before Treasury systems release funds.

There was probably $100 million, maybe $200 billion worth of zombie payments per year, which simply by enforcing that there be a payment code and an explanation for the payment, that the payment would not go out.
— Elon Musk

What DOGE Focused On

  • Stopping payments with no clear authorization.
  • Adding accountability to Treasury systems.
  • Exposing how hard it is to halt “auto-pilot” spending once it’s embedded.

Why He Wouldn’t Do It Again

Musk’s conclusion is blunt: the effort was costly, politically bruising, and came at the expense of his companies. He argues that cutting off money flows triggers intense backlash.

If you stop money going for political corruption, they will lash out big time. So they really want the money to keep flowing.
— Elon Musk

In other words, DOGE may have found savings, but it also invited a level of resistance he does not want to repeat.

AI, Robotics, and an Optional-Work Future

Musk’s view of AI’s trajectory is both optimistic and uneasy. He predicts that AI and robotics will eventually do almost any task people ask of them, which implies a future where work becomes optional—but not necessarily a future he would personally choose.

The AI and robots will be able to do anything that humans want them to do, essentially.
— Elon Musk
Work will be optional. I just want to separate out what I wish would happen versus what I predict will happen.
— Elon Musk

Key Distinctions He Makes

  • Prediction is not the same as preference.
  • AI progress is accelerating even if it feels risky.
  • Meaning and purpose may matter more when work is no longer required.

Starship and the “Evolutionary Hall of Fame”

When the conversation turns to Mars and long-term survival, Musk argues that fully reusable rockets are not just a technical milestone but a civilizational one. In his framing, becoming multi-planetary is among the most consequential events in the history of life.

If there are historians in the future, they’ll look back at Starship and say it was one of the most profound things that ever happened.
— Elon Musk
Life becoming multi-planetary is in the top 10.
— Elon Musk

Why Multi-Planetary Redundancy Matters

  • Reusable rockets make large-scale transport feasible.
  • Survival requires more than visits—sustainable settlement matters.
  • Redundancy protects civilization from single-planet catastrophes.

Fear, Sleep, and the Cost of Context Switching

Beyond politics and technology, the interview digs into daily life. Musk says he tries to stamp out irrational fears, aims for roughly six hours of sleep, and views constant task switching as a hidden drain on cognitive performance.

I try not to have irrational fears. If I find an irrational fear, I squelch it.
— Elon Musk
I tried having less than six hours sleep, but although I’m awake more hours per day, my cognitive function is reduced.
— Elon Musk
I try to segment the days so that there’s not too much context switching, because arguably fear is not the mind killer, context switching is.
— Elon Musk

Conclusion: A Single Thread Through Disparate Topics

Across DOGE, AI, and Mars, Musk’s emphasis is the same: reduce waste, maximize capability, and build resilience. He describes a future where AI changes what work means, rockets change what survival means, and governance determines how quickly either can happen. Whether or not you agree with his positions, the transcript makes one thing clear: he is thinking in terms of systems—payments, platforms, and planets—and aiming for the bottleneck in each.
